## Supervisor's Budget Message

This proposed 2027 Town of Riverhead budget framework puts taxpayers first by reducing the
General Fund levy target while protecting services, reserves, and long-term financial
stability. It follows a simple principle: taxpayers should not be overcharged to cover
avoidable inefficiency, but they also should not be handed a tax cut that creates a
larger problem in the next budget year.

The proposed 2027 framework is built around three goals:

1. Use the Town's 2025 operating surplus for one-time public priorities.
2. Reduce 2027 property-tax pressure without creating a recurring budget gap.
3. Reserve honestly for labor contracts, benefits, debt, and service needs.

The 2025 Annual Financial Report shows the General Fund ended 2025 with approximately
$33.4 million in total fund balance and approximately $29.7 million in unassigned fund
balance. Revenues and other sources exceeded expenditures and other uses by about
$5.0 million. This budget framework treats that $5.0 million as one-time capacity, not
as recurring revenue.

The plan therefore separates the budget into two different decisions:

- One-time surplus allocation: parks, vehicles, software, training, labor pressure,
  tax stabilization, and classification/compensation work.
- Recurring levy reduction: early retirement savings, vacancy control, software/process
  savings, avoided vehicle costs, cannabis revenue history, and procurement savings.

The proposed 2027 General Fund property-tax levy target is a 3.0 percent reduction from
the 2026 planning levy baseline used in the app. This should be treated as a policy target
that depends on adoption of the recurring savings package and careful use of the tax
stabilization fund.

This budget also recognizes that public finance is not just arithmetic. It is a trust
document. Residents should be able to see what is being funded, what is being reduced,
what is being reserved, and what assumptions must be proven before the Town relies on
them. The Town Board, union leadership, department heads, and residents should review
the early retirement model, labor reserve, tax stabilization fund, and department service
impacts collaboratively before this proposal becomes an official budget.

## Employee Benefits and Labor Pressure

The 2025 AFR shows General Fund employee benefits of approximately $18.8 million, up
from approximately $16.4 million in 2024. The 2027 proposal therefore includes a separate
labor pressure reserve rather than burying labor growth in departmental lines.

| Labor / Benefit Item | Planning Amount | Treatment |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Contract and labor pressure reserve | $1,200,000 | One-time stabilization reserve inside $5M surplus package |
| Early retirement planning-case recurring savings | $461,000 | Count only after payout/backfill/overtime analysis |
| Vacancy and position controls | $500,000 | Recurring savings target |
| Classification and compensation investments | $175,000 | Targeted title changes, compensation review, and limited headcount |
| Training and tuition programs | $150,000 | Retention, certifications, succession planning |

Contract context:

- CSEA 2027 is modeled elsewhere in the app as +2.5% plus $1,000 per FTE.
- PBA and SOA agreements run through 2026, making successor bargaining a 2027 risk.
- Salary savings from early retirement should not be booked until position refill,
  elimination, leave payout, and overtime assumptions are known.

## Capital, Stabilization, and One-Time Surplus Plan

| One-Time Use | Proposed Amount |
| --- | ---: |
| Parks and public spaces | $750,000 |
| Vehicles and fleet replacement | $525,000 |
| Software improvements | $150,000 |
| Training and tuition programs | $150,000 |
| Contract and labor pressure reserve | $1,200,000 |
| Tax stabilization fund | $2,000,000 |
| Classification and compensation investments | $175,000 |
| **Total one-time surplus plan** | **$4,950,000** |
| **Unallocated cushion** | **$50,000** |

Policy conditions:

1. The tax stabilization fund should be separately assigned and used only for levy
   smoothing, documented shocks, or transition support.
2. The labor pressure reserve should not hide recurring contract costs. Any recurring
   wage increase must appear in the 2028 baseline.
3. Parks, vehicles, software, training, and classification work should have project
   lists, owners, and completion dates.
4. No one-time surplus should fund permanent salary lines unless the next-year funding
   source is identified.
