## Practical Rebalance Suggestions

1. Hold police overtime, holiday pay, and buy-back accounts to monthly variance review instead of treating the new peak levels as the permanent baseline.
2. Separate one-off utility burdens like `Peconic Hockey electricity` from normal Town Hall operations so they can be challenged and offset transparently.
3. Review disposal and treatment costs in `ES1` and `ES5` first. Those utility and waste functions show some of the biggest recurring jumps.
4. Restore select fleet and field-support accounts for `Building`, `Code Enforcement`, and `Buildings & Grounds` where the Town is expecting stronger service delivery.
5. Keep consultant and special-event lines visible and purpose-bound, especially in `Finance`, `Community Development`, and `Water`.

## Best Candidates for an FY27 Rebalanced Plan

- Tighten:
  - Police holiday pay union
  - Police health insurance buy back
  - Tax collection postage
  - Community development special events
  - Sewer and scavenger waste disposal contracts
  - Water consulting and lab-fee growth

- Strengthen:
  - Building equipment and field support
  - Building / Code fleet replacement
  - Buildings & Grounds vehicle replacement
  - Senior transportation capital support
  - Preventive maintenance where cuts appear to defer rather than solve cost
